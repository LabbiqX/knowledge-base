## TL;DR
South Korea's $850M government initiative to roll out 76 AI-generated textbooks collapsed just 4 months after launch. The textbooks were full of errors, caused technical problems, and demanded more work from teachers instead of less. Over half of 4,095 participating schools opted out by October, and publishers invested $567M with no return.

---

## Key Points
- "AI Digital Textbook Promotion Plan" launched March 2025 with 12 publishing companies
- Promised personalized learning in math, English, and coding
- Textbooks full of embarrassing errors and technical problems
- Reclassified from mandatory to "supplemental materials" after 4 months
- Over 2,000 schools dropped out by mid-October
- Publishers formed emergency committee, filed constitutional petition
- Total investment: $567M by publishers, $850M government commitment
