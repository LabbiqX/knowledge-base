## TL;DR
Anthropic launched Claude Dispatch for Cowork, a mobile-controlled AI agent rivaling OpenClaw. OpenClaw's popularity is staggering — nearly 1,000 Tencent engineers lined up in Shenzhen to install it. The basic formula (LLM agent + local drive access + mobile messaging control) has proven irresistible. Anthropic's offering adds enterprise-grade guardrails while OpenClaw's lack of security boundaries remains both its weakness and strength.

---

## Key Points
- ~1,000 Tencent engineers lined up in Shenzhen to get OpenClaw installed — Chinese AI startups are "raising a lobster"
- OpenClaw's three foundations: LLM agent, local drive access, mobile messaging control
- Claude Dispatch is Anthropic's answer — same concept but with enterprise security
- Apple Silicon hardware is well-suited for local on-device AI inference, driving Mac Mini sales
- OpenClaw's lack of secure boundaries is simultaneously its greatest weakness and strength
- No company that could be sued would follow OpenClaw's permissive approach
