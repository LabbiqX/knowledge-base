## TL;DR
2025 was the year "vibe coding" (Collins Dictionary word of the year) transformed software engineering. AI now writes 30%+ of code at Google, "most of" it at Anthropic, and increasingly at Meta and Microsoft. But the revolution is messy: a study found AI coding tools decreased experienced developer productivity by 19%, Copilot users had 41% more bugs, and developer job postings dropped from 159K to 92.5K since 2023. CS students are the most pessimistic about their careers among all disciplines.

---

## Key Points
- "Vibe coding" = Collins 2025 word of the year, coined by Karpathy in February
- AI code generation: 30%+ at Google, 90% at Anthropic, growing at Meta/Microsoft
- Uplevel study: Copilot users weren't more efficient, had 41% more bugs
- Developer job postings: 159K (2023) → 92.5K (Nov 2025), while AI skill demand up 53%
- CS seniors most likely to be "very pessimistic" about careers among all disciplines
- "Everyone's confidence has increased, but so has their laziness" — Sriraam Raja
- Stack Overflow 2025: only 19.3% of devs don't use AI, but <3% highly trust it for accuracy
- Cognition (Devin AI) valued at $10B; non-technical people building apps
- Product managers suggest they can take on coding tasks, work without engineers
- "Well-rounded people are the ones that are going to have success" — not ticket-clearers
