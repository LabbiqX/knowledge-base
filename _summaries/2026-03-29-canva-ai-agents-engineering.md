## TL;DR
AI agents are fundamentally reshaping software engineering workflows. Canva engineers draft detailed instructions for agents that work overnight, delivering "hours and hours" of autonomous work. Senior engineers now describe their jobs as "largely review." Startup Cora's 6-person team produced work that would have required 20-30 engineers five years ago. However, scaling challenges persist and fewer than 1 in 10 organizations have redesigned jobs for AI adoption.

---

## Key Points
- Canva engineers draft instructions for AI agents that execute overnight; results often "really impressive"
- Senior engineers' role shifting to review, steering agents, and taking responsibility for final product
- "Precision of articulation" and "mastery of the domain" are critical for effective agent use
- Startup Cora: 6-person team = 20-30 engineers of 5 years ago, all code written by agents
- Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei calls this the "centaur phase" of software engineering
- Microsoft AI chief warns AI can handle "most professional tasks" in 12-18 months
- Only ~10% of organizations have redesigned jobs for AI adoption (Accenture survey)
- Key advice: treat agents like junior employees — tell them what to do, provide success criteria, give examples
- Alex Salazar (Arcade): "AI is improving at an exponential rate. And you, as a human, are not."
