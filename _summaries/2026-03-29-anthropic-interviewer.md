## TL;DR
Anthropic launched "Anthropic Interviewer," a Claude-powered tool that automatically conducts detailed interviews at scale. In a test with 1,250 professionals (1,000 general workforce, 125 scientists, 125 creatives), most expressed optimism about AI at work. Workers want to delegate routine tasks while preserving identity-defining work. Creatives use AI for productivity despite peer stigma. Scientists want AI partnership for hypothesis generation but don't yet trust it for core research.

---

## Key Points
- AI-powered interview tool conducts qualitative research at unprecedented scale
- All interview data publicly released on HuggingFace for researchers
- **General workforce:** Optimistic overall; want to preserve professional identity tasks, delegate routine work
- **Creatives:** Using AI despite peer judgment; anxiety about displacement and creative identity erosion
- **Scientists:** Desire AI for hypothesis generation and experiment design; actual use limited to writing and debugging
- Pessimistic topics: educational integration, artist displacement, security concerns
- Workers envision roles shifting to overseeing AI systems
- Tool uses separate AI analysis (Clio) to cluster emergent themes from unstructured interview data
