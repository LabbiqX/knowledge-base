## TL;DR
OpenClaw creator Peter Steinberger joined OpenAI to "bring agents to everyone." The project moves to an independent foundation (OpenAI-sponsored). Anthropic's cease-and-desist over the original "ClawdBot" name pushed the most viral agent project into its rival's arms. LangChain CEO Harrison Chase notes OpenClaw's power came from being "unhinged" — something no major lab can replicate.

---

## Key Points
- OpenClaw went from playground project (Nov 2025) to OpenAI acquisition in months
- Steinberger's goal: "build an agent that even my mum can use"
- Anthropic's cease-and-desist over "ClawdBot" naming pushed Steinberger toward OpenAI
- LangChain banned OpenClaw on company laptops due to security risks
- Chase's 3 takeaways: natural language interface, memory as enabler, code generation as general-purpose agency
- Coding agents are effectively general-purpose agents (code execution under the hood)
- Meta acquired Manus AI and Limitless AI in same period — consolidation accelerating
- Industry shift: "from what AI can say to what AI can do"
- The "safe enterprise OpenClaw" is now the central question for every platform vendor

## 🎯 Anahtar Çıkarım
> OpenAI's acquisition of OpenClaw marks the official end of the chatbot era and the beginning of the agent era. The industry's center of gravity has shifted from conversational AI to autonomous agents — and the race to build the "safe enterprise version" is now the defining competition.
