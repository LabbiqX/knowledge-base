## TL;DR
Sol Rashidi (former AWS, IBM, Estée Lauder exec) warns about "intellectual atrophy" — losing cognitive ability by outsourcing thinking to AI. She uses 6-8 AI tools daily but only for acceleration, never replacement. Key rule: never use AI for written communication (emails, keynotes), never copy-paste outputs, and always ask "Am I using this to accelerate work, or to do the work for me?"

---

## Key Points
- "Intellectual atrophy" = losing cognitive ability from outsourcing thinking to tech (like muscle atrophy)
- Uses AI for data processing so she can focus on patterns, insights, frameworks
- Never uses AI for emails, keynotes, or personal communication — "no exceptions"
- Key test: "Making me faster, but is it making me more capable? More productive, but more valuable?"
- Never copy-paste AI output — treat it as first draft always
- GenAI makes thinking generic since everyone uses same tools; edge comes from cognitive power
- Junior data scientist vs senior: same deliverable in half the time, but short-circuited research/verification
- "I'm paying for your brain and uniqueness, not to copy and paste"

## 🎯 Anahtar Çıkarım
> AI kullanımında altın kural: "accelerate, don't replace" — AI işi hızlandırmalı ama düşünme sürecini devralmamalı, yoksa herkesin aynı jenerik çıktıyı ürettiği bir dünyada rekabet avantajınız kaybolur.
