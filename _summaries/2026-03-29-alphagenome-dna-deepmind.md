## TL;DR
Google DeepMind's AlphaGenome is an AI model that can analyze one million letters of DNA code at a time, helping unravel the "dark genome" (the 98% of DNA that isn't genes but plays crucial roles in gene regulation). It can predict gene expression, gene splicing, and the impact of single-letter mutations — potentially transforming disease research, drug discovery, and cancer treatment.

---

## Key Points
- AlphaGenome analyzes up to 1 million letters of DNA code at once
- It can predict where genes are and what the "dark genome" influences (gene expression, splicing)
- Crucially predicts impact of changing even a single letter in genetic code
- Already being used by 3,000+ scientists for research into obesity, diabetes, cancer
- Can help identify which cancer mutations are driving tumors vs incidental
- Unlike LLMs, it's a "sequence-to-function model" trained on human and mouse cell experiment databases
- Still not perfect — less accurate for long-distance gene regulation (>100,000 letters away)
- Builds on DeepMind's Nobel Prize-winning AlphaFold work
