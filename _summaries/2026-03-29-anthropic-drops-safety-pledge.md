## TL;DR
Anthropic is dropping the central pledge of its Responsible Scaling Policy (RSP) — the 2023 commitment to never train an AI system without guaranteeing adequate safety measures in advance. The new policy commits to matching or surpassing competitors' safety efforts and delaying development only if Anthropic considers itself the AI leader AND sees significant catastrophe risks. The change reflects competitive pressure and the failure of hoped-for regulations to materialize.

---

## Key Points
- Original 2023 RSP: categorical bar on training models above certain capability without safety measures in place
- New RSP: no longer requires guaranteed safety measures before training; commits to transparency and matching competitors
- Jared Kaplan: "We didn't feel it made sense to make unilateral commitments if competitors are blazing ahead"
- New commitments: "Frontier Safety Roadmaps" and "Risk Reports" every 3-6 months
- Context: Anthropic raised $30B at $380B valuation; 10x annual revenue growth
- Expected federal regulations never materialized; Trump Administration endorsed let-it-rip approach
- AI evaluation science proved more complicated than expected — "bright red lines" became "fuzzy gradients"
- METR policy director: "This is more evidence that society is not prepared for potential catastrophic AI risks"
