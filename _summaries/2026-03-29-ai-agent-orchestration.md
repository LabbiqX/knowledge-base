## TL;DR
Enterprise AI is shifting from "can agents work for us?" to "can agents work together?" Orchestration across multi-agent systems is becoming the critical differentiator. Early platforms (Salesforce MuleSoft, UiPath Maestro, IBM Watsonx) provide observability dashboards, but will evolve into risk management tools with agent assessments, reliability scoring, and automated guardrail management — moving from human-in-the-loop to human-on-the-loop.

---

## Key Points
- Without orchestration, agents get "misunderstandings" that reduce action quality and risk hallucinations/security incidents
- Phase 1: Software-based observability dashboards to see all agentic actions across enterprise
- Evolution toward risk management: agent assessments, policy recommendations, proactive reliability scoring
- "Ticket exhaustion" problem: semi-automated systems overwhelm humans with approval requests
- Third-party orchestration can manage approval tickets and eventually eliminate persistent human-in-the-loop
- Human evaluators becoming designers: from human-in-the-loop to human-on-the-loop
- No-code agent builder platforms democratizing agent creation
- Agent-first automation stacks "dramatically outperform" hybrid stacks
- Key advice: "You can't orchestrate what you can't see clearly"
