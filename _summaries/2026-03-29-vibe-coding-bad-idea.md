## TL;DR
A Linux expert with 15+ years experience argues vibe coding is terrible for three reasons: (1) it removes you from the problem space and you learn nothing, (2) you're operating blind on code you don't understand, and (3) AI can't improve a codebase over time. He argues that outsourcing tough challenges to AI limits your potential to AI's capabilities, creates dependency, and robs you of the competency edge that matters now more than ever.

---

## Key Points
- **Learning gap:** Offloading challenges to AI robs you of discovering new tools/approaches (e.g., property-based testing)
- **Operating blind:** "Vibe" means going with what "feels" good — unacceptable where health, finance, or safety matter
- **No maintenance intelligence:** AI lacks motivation for the Boy Scout Rule (leave code better than you found it)
- AI adds complexity and spaghetti code rather than simplifying
- "You trade intelligence for dependence and become weaker"
- Competency through firsthand experience is the only way to solve hard problems
- AI inherently hallucinates — always needs a skilled handler intimate with the project
- "Your potential never goes beyond the AI's capabilities — the same as everyone else"
- Quality comes at a price: time, effort, expertise, and dedication
