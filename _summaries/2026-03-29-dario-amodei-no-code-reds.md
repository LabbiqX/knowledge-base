## TL;DR
At the NYT Dealbook Summit, Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei took a subtle shot at OpenAI and Google, saying Anthropic has never needed to declare "code reds" in response to competitor releases. He attributes this to Anthropic's enterprise focus over consumer AI, optimizing for coding, intellectual activities, and scientific ability rather than engagement. He called some competitors "YOLO" in their spending.

---

## Key Points
- Anthropic has issued no "code reds" while OpenAI and Google traded them
- Enterprise focus gives "privileged position" — less competitive panic
- Enterprise AI priorities: coding, high intellectual activities, scientific ability (vs. consumer engagement)
- Expanding beyond coding into finance, biomedical, retail, energy
- Skeptical of massive AI spending by Google, OpenAI, Meta: "some players are YOLO"
- "Real dilemma" in uncertainty of how quickly economic value will grow
- Claude Opus 4.5 recently released as most advanced model
