## TL;DR
Microsoft AI CEO Mustafa Suleyman warns that competing at the top level of AI will require "hundreds of billions of dollars" over the next 5-10 years for infrastructure, hardware, and talent. He describes Microsoft as a "modern construction company" building gigawatts of AI infrastructure. Major tech companies are engaged in an intense AI talent war with $100M+ compensation packages.

---

## Key Points
- "Hundreds of billions" needed over 5-10 years for AI leadership
- Costs beyond hardware: fierce competition for AI researchers/engineers
- Meta's Zuckerberg: offered $100M signing bonuses to recruit OpenAI employees
- Google: $2.4B deal to hire Windsurf CEO Varun Mohan for DeepMind
- Microsoft quietly hired 2 dozen Google DeepMind employees
- Meta investing $14B in AI Superintelligence Labs
- Zuckerberg: "prefer to risk misspending a couple hundred billion" than fall behind
- Suleyman's goal: make Microsoft "self-sufficient" in frontier models
