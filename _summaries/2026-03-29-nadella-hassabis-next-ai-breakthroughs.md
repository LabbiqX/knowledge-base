## TL;DR
Both Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella and Google DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis believe the current AI era is not the final one. Nadella says "we are one innovation away from the entire regime changing" — pointing to new model architectures beyond transformers. Hassabis says AI needs "one or two more breakthroughs" in reasoning, memory, and "world model" ideas, placing AGI at 5-10 years away.

---

## Key Points
- Nadella: evolution went from pre-training scale → post-training → reasoning → RL, with constant innovation
- "It's not just one transformer model" — multiple architectures and approaches contributing
- Nadella hints at fundamentally new, more efficient model architectures beyond transformers
- Hassabis: scaling must be pushed to maximum, but returns are diminishing due to finite public training data
- Required breakthroughs: reasoning, memory, and "world model" ideas
- Hassabis puts AGI at 50% odds by 2030, 5-10 years away
- Current systems are "a key component of the final AGI system" but possibly not the entire answer

> The two most influential AI leaders agree: current transformer-based AI is necessary but not sufficient for AGI. One to two more fundamental breakthroughs — likely in reasoning, memory, and world models — are needed to change the entire regime.
