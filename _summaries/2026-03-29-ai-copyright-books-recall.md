## TL;DR
Stanford and Yale researchers found that major LLMs (GPT-4.1, Gemini 2.5 Pro, Grok 3, Claude 3.7 Sonnet) can reproduce copyrighted books near-verbatim. Claude reproduced books at 95.8% accuracy; Gemini reproduced Harry Potter at 76.8%. This undermines the AI industry's defense that models "learn" from data rather than storing it.

---

## Key Points
- Four frontier LLMs tested for copyrighted content reproduction
- Claude: 95.8% accuracy reproducing entire books, including "1984" at 94%+
- Some extractions required Best-of-N jailbreaking technique
- Findings could be "massive legal liability" costing billions in judgments
- AI companies continue to argue models don't "contain" copies
- Fair use doctrine is the industry's primary legal defense
- Multiple copyright lawsuits already in courts
