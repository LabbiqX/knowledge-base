## TL;DR
A Work AI Institute report (with Notre Dame, Harvard, UC Santa Barbara researchers) warns that generative AI creates an "illusion of expertise" — workers feel smarter and more productive while their underlying skills erode. Early-career workers are most exposed as AI replaces traditional apprenticeship tasks. Companies measuring AI adoption by usage metrics incentivize shallow use over real learning, creating "cognitive debt" instead of "cognitive dividend."

---

## Key Points
- AI creates either "cognitive dividend" (used as partner with expertise) or "cognitive debt" (used as shortcut)
- Using AI to beat the "blank page" strips away the messy work of wrestling with ideas
- "The more you poke holes in it, the more it feels yours" — skipping this process atrophies skills
- Early-career workers most at risk: traditional apprenticeship tasks being automated away
- Red flag: companies stack-ranking employees by AI tool clicks as productivity metric
- Companies should measure real outcomes (quality, satisfaction, innovation) not usage frequency
- Three key questions: What stays human? Where is AI adjacent to expertise? What are you measuring?
- "AI does not magically transform you as a leader — it amplifies what already exists"
