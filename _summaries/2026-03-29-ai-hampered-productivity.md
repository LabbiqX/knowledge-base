## TL;DR
A METR study found that experienced software developers using AI tools (Cursor Pro, Claude 3.5/3.7) completed tasks 19% slower than without AI, despite predicting a 24% speedup. The study highlights that AI's impressive raw capabilities don't automatically translate to productivity gains for experienced developers working on familiar codebases.

---

## Key Points
- 16 developers, 246 tasks, randomized AI/no-AI assignment
- Developers predicted 24% speed boost but experienced 19% slowdown
- Main time sinks: retrofitting AI output to existing context, debugging AI code, prompt writing
- AI lacks the deep project context experienced developers already possess
- MIT report: only 5% of 300 AI deployments achieved rapid revenue acceleration
- Nobel laureate Acemoglu: only 4.6% of US economy tasks will be more efficient with AI
- Study authors caution against generalizing — small sample, brand-new tools
