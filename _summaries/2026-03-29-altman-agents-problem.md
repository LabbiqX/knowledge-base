## TL;DR
OpenAI CEO Sam Altman publicly acknowledged AI agents are creating real problems, revealing models are "beginning to find critical vulnerabilities" in computer security systems. OpenAI is hiring a Head of Preparedness ($555K + equity) to address risks in cybersecurity, biosecurity, and self-improving AI. Altman also acknowledged AI's potential psychological impact, following lawsuits alleging ChatGPT involvement in teen suicides.

---

## Key Points
- AI models discovering critical security vulnerabilities — a double-edged sword
- Head of Preparedness role: $555K + equity, covering cybersecurity/biosecurity/self-improving AI
- Position vacant after multiple safety team leadership changes in 2024-2025
- Chinese state-sponsored hackers used Claude Code to target ~30 global entities
- Mental health concerns: lawsuits over ChatGPT involvement in teen suicides
- Reports of AI chatbots feeding users' delusions and conspiracy theories
- Altman calls it "a stressful job" with immediate deep-end challenges
