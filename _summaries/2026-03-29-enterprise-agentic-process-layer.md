## TL;DR
85% of enterprises want to become agentic within 3 years, but 76% admit their operations can't support it. Only 19% use multi-agent systems today. The gap isn't technology — it's siloed teams (54%), lack of coordination (44%), and unoptimized processes. AI agents need process intelligence (how the business actually runs) to deliver ROI, not just better models.

---

## Key Points
- Celonis survey: 1,600+ global business leaders; 82% believe AI fails without business context understanding
- Only 6% cite resistance to change; real blockers are structural (silos, coordination)
- 93% of process leaders say optimization is as much about people/culture as tools
- Process intelligence = shared operational language grounding AI decisions in reality
- "You can't bolt AI onto a broken process" — operating model must evolve with tooling
- 63% use process optimization for proactive risk management; 58% for faster decisions
- Supply chain: 66% view process optimization as critical business-wide initiative
- Winners won't have the most sophisticated AI — they'll have the clearest operational picture

## 🎯 Anahtar Çıkarım
> The agentic AI readiness gap isn't about models or tools — it's about processes. Organizations that build operational visibility and process intelligence will succeed; those layering AI on fragmented processes will get expensive pilots that don't scale.
