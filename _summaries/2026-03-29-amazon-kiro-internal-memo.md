## TL;DR
Amazon sent an internal memo directing engineers to adopt its proprietary Kiro development system and avoid third-party AI coding tools. The directive affects OpenAI's Codex, Anthropic's Claude Code, and Cursor — despite Amazon investing $8B in Anthropic and $38B cloud deal with OpenAI. Over 250,000 developers have used Kiro in its first 3 months.

---

## Key Points
- Memo signed by senior AWS/e-commerce executives DeSantis and Treadwell
- "We do not plan to support additional third party AI development tools"
- OpenAI Codex previously designated "Do Not Use" internally
- Claude Code briefly restricted, reversed after reporter inquiry
- Kiro: 250,000+ developers, 300M+ requests in 3 months
- Spec-driven development: generates specs, design docs, task lists alongside code
- AI code assistant market projected: $5.5B (2024) → $47.3B (2034)
