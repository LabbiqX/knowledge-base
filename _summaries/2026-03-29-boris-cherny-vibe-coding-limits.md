## TL;DR
Boris Cherny, creator of Claude Code, says vibe coding works well for throwaway code and prototypes but falls short for maintainable, critical code. For important tasks, he pairs with AI — generating plans, iterating in small steps, and writing by hand for parts where he has strong technical opinions. He notes models are still "not great at coding" but the improvement trajectory from last year's autocomplete to today's agents is "insane."

---

## Key Points
- Vibe coding good for: throwaway code, prototypes, non-critical path code
- Not suitable for: maintainable code, security-critical systems, large codebases
- Cherny's workflow for critical code: AI generates plan → iterate small steps → manual cleanup
- Writes by hand for parts where he has strong technical opinions
- Models still "not great at coding" but improving rapidly — "worst it's ever going to be"
- AI coding went from "type-ahead autocomplete" to agents in just one year
- Dario Amodei: Claude writing 90% of code at Anthropic
- Google CEO Pichai: AI writing 30%+ of new code at Google
- Andrew Ng: developers can write software "barely looking at the code"
