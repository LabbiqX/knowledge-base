## TL;DR
Integral AI, founded by ex-Google veteran Jad Tarifi in Tokyo, claims to have built the world's first AGI-capable model. Their definition of AGI requires autonomous skill learning without datasets/human intervention, safe/reliable mastery, and human-equivalent energy efficiency. They claim robots learned new skills without supervision, but evidence is hard to verify independently.

---

## Key Points
- Founded by Jad Tarifi after 10 years at Google, based in Tokyo for Japan's robotics leadership
- AGI definition: autonomous learning + safe mastery + energy efficiency matching humans
- Claims system mirrors multi-layered neocortex for conscious thought
- Robot trials show skills learned without human supervision
- Goal: scale toward "embodied superintelligence"
- Claims are hard to independently substantiate
- Part of broader AGI claims trend alongside Sapient Intelligence, DeepSeek
