## TL;DR
Multi-agent AI research reveals that software project failures stem from batch size, not human factors. Large batches of work fail whether humans or AI agents execute them. The same coordination complexity and integration failures occur regardless of who writes the code, proving that Continuous Delivery principles matter even when AI is the developer.

---

## Key Points
- Traditional thinking blames human factors (laziness, politics) for software failures, but multi-agent AI experiments show the same failure patterns
- Software delivery has a "gravity" that increases with batch size — larger batches = more failures
- Jeremy McEntire's experiment showed coordination complexity outweighs benefits when AI agent swarms handle large tasks
- The same classes of failure occur in the same places regardless of whether humans or AI agents do the work
- Continuous Delivery (small batches, frequent integration) remains essential even with AI-written code
- The analogy: blaming people for batch-size failures is like believing gravity is willpower

---

## 🎯 Anahtar Çıkarım
> Batch size, not human inadequacy, is the root cause of software project failures. AI agents fail in the same ways human teams do when given large, complex tasks — proving that Continuous Delivery principles are physics, not people management.
