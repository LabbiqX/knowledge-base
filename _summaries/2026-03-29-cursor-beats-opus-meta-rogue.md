## TL;DR
Cursor released Composer 2, its in-house coding model that beats Claude Opus 4.6 on Terminal-Bench 2.0 (61.7% vs 58%) at 1/10th the cost ($0.50/$2.50 vs $5/$25 per million tokens). Nvidia announced a coalition with Cursor, Mistral, Perplexity to build shared base models. Congress unveiled the 300-page Trump America AI Act to preempt state-level AI laws.

---

## Key Points
- Composer 2: 61.7% on Terminal-Bench 2.0 (beats Opus 4.6 at 58%), competitive with GPT-5.4 Thinking (63.9%)
- Pricing: $0.50/$2.50 per million tokens vs Opus's $5/$25 — 10x cheaper
- Trained exclusively on code data with RL on long-horizon coding tasks
- Nvidia coalition (Cursor, Mistral, Perplexity, LangChain, Black Forest Labs) to build shared base models
- OpenAI merging three apps into one
- Anthropic dropped prices twice in one week
- Congress released 300-page bill to replace every state-level AI law
