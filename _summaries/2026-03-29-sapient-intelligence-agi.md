## TL;DR
William Chen and Guan Wang, two 22-year-old friends from Michigan, turned down a multi-million dollar offer from Elon Musk's xAI to pursue a brain-inspired AI architecture called Hierarchical Reasoning Model (HRM). Their tiny 27M parameter model outperformed GPT-4, Claude, and DeepSeek on abstract reasoning benchmarks like ARC-AGI and Sudoku-Extreme, without chain-of-thought prompting.

---

## Key Points
- Built OpenChat at Tsinghua University using RL for LLMs before DeepSeek made it popular
- Turned down xAI recruitment to pursue new architecture beyond transformers
- HRM uses two-part recurrent structure modeled on human brain's slow/fast thinking
- 27M parameter model outperformed frontier models on reasoning tasks
- Claims far less hallucination than traditional LLMs
- Working on scaling HRM as general-purpose reasoning engine
- Thesis: AGI won't come from bigger transformers, but smaller, more efficient architectures
