## TL;DR
Mark Cuban warns that OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Microsoft, Meta, and Perplexity are overspending in an AI arms race that mirrors the 1990s search engine wars — where Google ultimately won and others became irrelevant. He predicts the AI competition could "pop just like that" with any new technology breakthrough, and current massive data center investments may become obsolete.

---

## Key Points
- Cuban compares AI race to pre-Google search engine competition — "effectively winner-take-all"
- Warns of AI bubble from competitive overspending
- Massive data center investments may be disrupted by future technology improvements
- "They anticipate for at least another decade spending every penny they have"
- Disruption will come from something "incredible" no one sees coming
- Infrastructure costs may become obsolete as technology improves over 10 years
- Cuban believes current spending levels are "ripe for disruption"
