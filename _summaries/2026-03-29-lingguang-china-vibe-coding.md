## TL;DR
LingGuang, a Chinese vibe-coding app by Ant Group (Alibaba affiliate), hit 2M+ downloads in days, briefly crashing from demand. Its standout feature is a real-time "AGI camera" that analyzes physical scenes and generates mini-apps in ~60 seconds from plain-language prompts. In hands-on testing, it beat ChatGPT on UX immediacy — particularly real-time product comparison at a supermarket and on-the-fly video generation from camera captures.

---

## Key Points
- Built by Ant Group (Alipay owner), 2M+ downloads in first week
- "AGI camera" analyzes real-time scenes — recognized startup founder from video, compared products on shelf
- Real-time voice mode for follow-up questions in Chinese
- Flash app feature: builds mini-apps in ~60 seconds from prompts
- On-the-fly video generation from camera captures (20 seconds)
- UX advantage over ChatGPT: capture → edit → generate in single workflow
- Drawback: AGI camera sessions don't save (can't revisit later)
- ChatGPT still better for complex tasks and code visibility
- Flash app crashed from overuse at launch
